## Human-Written Article Angle

AI Malware Analysis Needs Validation Gaps, Not Autonomous Verdicts

## Core Argument

The cybersecurity community should measure AI-assisted malware-analysis tools by
how well they expose uncertainty, not by how confidently they produce labels.

## Points To Cover

- Static analysis is useful but incomplete.
- Dynamic execution proves behavior but can miss branch-specific logic.
- Models are good at summarizing evidence and proposing hypotheses.
- Models are weak at source authority, runtime proof, and edge-case certainty.
- A good workflow should output confirmed facts, likely hypotheses, and
  validation gaps separately.
- Detection engineering should consume evidence, not model confidence alone.
